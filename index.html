Key Learnings
01
Data Quality is the Foundation
No matter how good your analysis is, dirty data produces unreliable insights. Spending time on proper data cleaning pays dividends in every subsequent step of the project.
02
EDA Means Answering Business Questions
Visualizations are only useful when they answer specific business questions. The best analysts translate data into decisions — not just charts.
03
Segmentation Drives Strategy
Treating all customers the same is a mistake. 10 Champions drive 31.8% of revenue — a fact that completely changes how a business should allocate its marketing budget.
04
Statistics Gives Confidence to Act
Anyone can say "CC users seem to spend more." But proving it at p=0.0037 with a 95% confidence interval gives a business the confidence to actually invest in that insight.
05
Storytelling is the Last Mile
Data without communication is wasted. The ability to craft a narrative — objective → analysis → conclusion → call to action — separates a data analyst from a data processor.
06
Every Dataset Has a Business Story
What looked like a simple sales CSV turned into insights about retention crises, payment behavior, regional opportunities, and statistically proven fulfillment risks.
